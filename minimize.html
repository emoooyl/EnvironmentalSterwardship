Energy-Efficient Production

  • Manufacturing factories can switch over to renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, or hydropower to reduce their carbon footprint.
  • Companies and manufacturers can also switch over to efficient production processes to minimize energy and material use. This lowers the company's greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Additionally, companies can obtain certifications such as Energy Star and EPEAT which indicate the companies contributions to following environmentally friendly practices.

Design for Longevity and Repairability

  • Manufacturers can design devices with durable and long-lasting components that last long periods to reduce the amount of replacements.
  • Companies can create designs with modular parts that can easily be upgraded or replaced such as our RAM or storage which allows their customers to extend the life of their devices without having to constantly replace the whole unit.
  • Manufacturers can design devices that are easy to repair with parts and tools to help encourage repair over replacement.

Reducing E-waste by Recycling

  • Technology companies can introduce "take-back" programs to their customers which allows them to return their device for recycling or refurbishment in return for a discount on a newer model, or in-store credit. This ensures that the device or the parts are recycled to make new ones or handed down to a new customer.
  • Manufactures can establish "closed-loop" recycling programs where the materials from old devices are recycled and reused to make new ones. This ensures that devices with components that are still good quality can be reused instead of leaving it as a one-use part.
